Management of Anaphylaxis (Adult)

-        ABCDE

-        High-flow O2

 

Diagnosis:

o   Look for

§  Acute onset

§  A, B or C compromise

§  Skin changes (common)

§  Trigger

·        Eg recent drug administration; known nut allergy

 

-        ADRENALINE

·        Give IM from epi-pen if available

·        Alternatively give IM

§  0.5mg ie 0.5ml (500 micrograms) of 1:1000 adrenaline IM

§  Repeat after 5 minutes if no improvement

 

-        Call for help

-        IV saline – 500ml over 15 mins

-        10mg Chlorphenamine IV

-        200mg hydrocortisone IV

-        Raise patient’s legs

-        Monitor BP, sats and ECG

 

**Notes – ADRENALINE:

 

In anaphylaxis, 0.5mls of 1:1000 adrenaline is given IM.

In cardiac arrest 1mg Adrenaline is given IV after the 3rd shock of VT/VF arrest and can be repeated every 3-5 minutes.

In PEA/aystole arrests adrenaline is given 1mg IV as soon as IV access is obtained.
