Glasgow Coma Scale

-          Scored out of 15

-          Always report individual components of the score

·         Eg this patient has a GCS of 13 consisting of E3, V4, M6

 

-          GCS 3 = lowest score

-          GCS 8 = unlikely to be protecting their airway. ?intubate

-          Any ↓ in GCS is significant – GCS 14 is not OK!

·         That said, any drunk in A+E is likely to be confused and therefore 14 is the best GCS they could have. However you can’t dismiss this – they may still have pathology

 

-          Always test both sides and use the patient’s BEST RESPONSE

 

-          Painful stimuli should be elicited above the clavicle

·         Cranial nerve distribution (in case high cervical lesion)

·         Also should be peripheral to make sure it is not a reflex jerk – ie the patient is truly localising

·         Test both sides

·         Trapezius squeeze

·         Supra-orbital pressure

§  NOT sternal rub – litigation for bruising; central – could be reflex

§  Nail-bed pressure doesn’t allow localisation

 

-          Assess motor by asking patient to obey a command

·         Can you touch your nose for me? Hold up 2 fingers?

·         Or stick your tongue out (if ?cervical injury; tongue supplied by hypoglossal cranial nerve)

How to assess GCS

 

Always start with EYE OPENING.

Then depending on how they respond, move onto either verbal (eg if they are awake) or motor (if they needed a painful stimuli to open their eyes).

 

Patient awake in the bed

 

-       Check Eye opening - if open spontaneously or to speech

-       Move on to verbal

-       If orientated, ask them to touch their nose

-       If not orientated, tell them “this might be a bit uncomfortable” – squeeze their trapezius and assess their response to pain

 

Semi-conscious patient

 

-       “Hello sir, can you open your eyes for me?”

-       If eyes do not open in response to voice

-       Squeeze trapezius both sides – do they open their eyes?

-       Repeat trapezius squeeze and monitor motor response

-       Then assess Verbal response - are they making any sound?

 

‘Neuro obs’ consist of monitoring GCS, pupils ± neuro exam (tone and reflexes). If a patient has ↓GCS you can ask the nurses to do these hourly.

 

Further investigations may include:

-       Bloods – FBC, GLUCOSE, LFTs, U+Es

-       CT head

-       ECG
