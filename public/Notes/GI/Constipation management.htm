Management of Constipation

 

NB: Laxatives are contraindicated in obstruction. Need to exclude obstruction before prescribing.

 

1)     Look for cause

 

-        Mechanical obstruction

-        Hypothyroidism

-        Hypocalcaemia

-        Immobility

-        Dehydration

-        Drugs esp opiates (incl cocodamol). Also verapamil, ondansetron.

-        Stop iron and calcium supplements

 

2)     Investigate

 

-        PR!

-        Check U+Es

-        Calcium

-        TFTs

-        Glucose

-        AXR if  ?obstruction

 

3)     Management

 

Depends on PR findings:

Ø  Hard faeces – faecal softener eg lactulose

Ø  Soft faeces – stimulant eg senna

Ø  Impaction – glycerol suppository then enema eg phosphate enema

Ø  Empty rectum – AXR ?obstruction

 

Management of…

 

-        Acute constipation

·        Senna 2-4 tabs nocte

 

Chronic constipation

Ø Increase fluid

Ø Exercise

-        High fibre

·        Fybogel (ispaghula husk) 1 sachet BD + good fluid intake

·        If ineffective add:

§  Senna 2-4 tabs nocte (short term only)

§  Or lactulose 15ml BD

§  Or movicol 1-3 sachet

 

-        Can use all 3 if not effective. Movicol tends to be the most potent.

 

-        Opiod induced constipation

·        Senna + lactulose

 

 

Pharmacology of Laxatives

 

Aka Purgatives – accelerate the passage of food through the intestine

 

Purgatives include…

 

-        Laxatives, faecal softeners, stimulant purgatives

 

Bulk & Osmotic Laxatives

 

Bulk laxatives

 

-        Polysaccharide polymers that are not broken down so therefore add bulk to faeces

-        Promote peristalsis and increase faecal consistency

-        Eg Methylcellulose, agar, bran, ispaghula husk (fybogel)

-        May take several days to work but no major side-effects

-        Can also just recommend Branflakes/wheatabix!

 

Osmotic Laxatives

 

-        Eg lactulose; saline purgatives.

-        Lactulose is a semi-synthetic combination of fructose and galactose that is poorly absorbed, and so draws fluid into the abdominal lumen to soften faeces.

-        It takes 2-3 days to work.

-        Works as a faecal softener

-        Esp good in hepatic encaphalopahty – reduces growth of ammonia producing bacteria

-        Given as a thick sticky liquid – tastes horrible

-        s/e – flatulence; cramps; diarrhoea and electrolyte disturbance; tolerance can develop.

 

-        Salt purgatives consist of poorly-absorbable salts (magnesium sulphate and magnesium hydroxide) that therefore draw increased volumes of fluid into the gut lumen. This increases transfer through the small intestine, and leads to increased volumes entering the colon → purgation.

-        Abdominal cramps can occur; salt doses are too small to have adverse systemic effects but should still be avoided in children and patients with renal failure.

 

Faecal softeners

 

-        Docusate sodium – surface active compound that acts like a detergent in the GIT to produce softer faeces

-        Also has a weak stimulant effect

-        Also liquid paraffin (although this is seldom used now).

 

Stimulant Laxatives

 

-        Act mainly by increasing electrolyte and hence water secretion by the muscosa

-        also increase peristalsis - ?by stimulating enteric nerves

-        s/e abdominal cramping

-        May produe atony with long term use

·        ‘tolerance’ can develop whereby the patient develops atonic colon in absences of laxative and therefore becomes ‘dependent’

-        Stimulant laxatives should not be given if ?obstruction!

-        Also avoid in active colitis.

 

Bisacodyl – acts very rapidly if given by suppository

 

Senna – anthroquinone laxative; active component of drug directly stimulates the myenteric plexus to increase peristalsis and thus defaecation.

 

Also:

 

Domperidone and Metoclopramide – increase gastric emptying – it increases LES tone whilst relaxing the pylorus and stimulating duodenal peristalsis. Mechanism of action is unknown. Useful in chronic reflux disease and in disorders of gastric emptying. They do not cause purgation (laxative effect) overall.
