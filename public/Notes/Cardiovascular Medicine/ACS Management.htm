Acute Coronary Syndromes: Management

MONAC:

-        Morphine IV 5mg (+metoclopramide 10mg IV)

-        Oxygen

-        Nitrates

-        ASPIRIN 300mg – unless contraindicated or given already (GP/ambulance)

-        Clopidogrel 300mg

 

ECG!

è If ST elevation or new LBBB = see STEMI Management

è Otherwise see NSTEMI management

 

Definition of an MI = +ve troponin plus at least 1 of

 

·        Ischaemic symptoms (chest pain)

·        ECG changes

·        Confirmation on angiography

 

 

 

Management of STEMI

 

-        Primary Percutaneous coronary Intervention

·        First line treatment

·        Phone the cath lab and fax the ECG

·        If Cardiac Reperfusion Centre is at a different hospital you need to dial 999 and arrange ambulance transfer

 

-        If going for PPCI give

·        Herapin 5000 units SC (1/2 dose if CKD)

·        Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitor

·        Metoprolol 5-15mg IV (withhold if <65bpm or systolic <105)

 

-        Only give thrombolysis if logistic reasons mean PPCI is too far away. And must check thrombolysis is not contraindicated.

 

Management of NSTEMI

 

-        Measure troponin

·        If –ve, re-test at 12 hours

§  If –ve at 12 hours review ECG and history and consider discharge

 

If +ve troponin

 

-        Monitor ECG

-        LMW heparin - Enoxaparin 1mg/kg twice daily

-        Continue aspirin at 75mg

-        Continue clopidogrel at 75mg

-        Nitrates for angina chest pain

 

If chest pain continues – refer to CCU

If no further pain – refer to cardiology (ETT, angioplasty)

 

Secondary prevention

 

-        Lifestyle factors – weight loss, smoking, exercise, diet

-        Statin – 40mg Simvastatin; 80mg Atorvostatin if +ve troponin

-        ACEi or A2RB

-        Aspirin

-        Clopidogrel (usu continue for 3 months although duration varies eg longer if drug eluting stent)

-        Consider B-blockers
