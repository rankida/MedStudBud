Marfan’s syndrome

-        Inherited connective tissue disorder

-        Autosomal dominant (chromosome 15)

 

Clinical Features:

 

-        Disproportionately tall with long limbs – arm span greater than their height; limbs are out of proportion to their torso.

-        Usu thin

-        Arachnodactyly – long spindly fingers

-        High arched palate in mouth

-        Hyper-mobile joints

-        Aortic regurgitation ± aneurysm ± dissection (à Echo!)

-        ↑risk of pneumothorax

-        ↑ risk of lens dislocation, high myopia and acute angle-closure glaucoma

-        ↑ risk of cauda equina syndrome

-        Striae  - sacral

-        Pectus excavatum
