Aortic Dissection

-        Tear in the intimal lining of the aorta à high pressure blood enters arterial wall creating a false lumen à haematoma separates layers à Media is pulled off the intima à can extend in either direction

-        If extends into endocardium can à aortic regurgitation and cardiac tamponade

-        Most commonly affects ascending aorta

-        May occur in existing aneurysm

 

Clinical Features:

 

-        Thoracic aortic dissection is an important differential for chest pain

o   Clinical examination differentiates dissection from ACS

 

-        Dissection pain is tearing in nature

·        ± radiates to the back

-        Differential pulses

-        Aortic regurgitation (murmur)

-        Cardiac tamponade

·        Soft heart sounds, pulsus paradoxus, kausmaul’s sign (JVP↑ on inspiration)

 

-        As the dissection spreads sequential branches of the aorta are affected resulting is serious and diverse clinical features:

·        Carotid artery à Stroke/hemiplegia

·        Left subclavian à Unequal arm pulses/BP

·        Left subclavian à Acute limb ischaemia

·        Anterior spinal artery à Paraplegia

·        Renal artery à Anuria/Acute renal failure

 

Type A [1]

-        Most common type ~70%

-        Dissection involves the ascending aorta

·        Although tear can start anywhere

-        Emergency surgery!!!

·        call cardiothoracic surgeon

 

Type B [1]

-        Ascending aorta is not involved

-        Conservative treatment is first line

-        However often require surgery

 

-        Management

 

-        Cross match 10 units

-        ECG – exclude ACS

-        CXR – may show widened mediastinum

-        CT/MRI or transoesophageal echo to confirm diagnosis

-        ITU

-        Control BP – systolic <110 eg labetolol
