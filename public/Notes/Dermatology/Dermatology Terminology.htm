Dermatology: Descriptive Terminology

-        Macule – small (<1cm), flat, any colour eg freckles, lentigo maligna

-        Patch – large, smooth area of discolouration

-        Papule – small (<1cm), raised eg lichen planus

-        Nodule – a large papule eg tumour, SCC

-        Plaque – raised, flat, eg chronic plaque psoriasis

-        Vesicle – small blister; fluid-filled papule eg acute eczema

-        Bullae – large blister; fluid-filled eg bullous pemphigoid

-        Pustule – pus-filled blister eg pustular psoriasis

-        Abscess – localised collection of pus

-        Telangectasia – prominent cutaneous blood vessels; dilated capillaries

-        Scar – post-traumatic fibrous (granulation) tissue

-        Lichenification – thickening of the skin; prominent skin markings; due to chronic rubbing eg in eczema

-        Weal – raised, smooth, urticated bump ‘solid blister’; smooth oedematous papule

-        Petechiae – small red/purple spots that do not blanch (due to capillary bleeding). Caused by thrombocytopaenia, meningitis, DIC, vasculitis.

-        Purpura – large petechiae (3mm-1cm)

-        Ecchymoses – a bruise (>1cm)

-        Ulcer – break in the epidermis eg Herpes

-        Erosion – ulcer due to trauma

-        Scale – dead skin (scaling = hyperkeratosis)

-        Crust – dried hard exudates eg infection

-        Cyst – fluid-filled lump, and so if fluctuant; papule or nodule that contains fluid; Cf: blister eg breast cyst in young lady

-        Excoriation – a scratch

-        Fissure – think crack in epidermis

-        Fungating – describes a large tumour erupting like a mushroom

-        Erythrodermic - >90% of body is red

Distribution of lesions:

-        Discoid/nummular – coin shaped

-        Annular – ring shaped eg ring worm

-        Polycylic – multiple overlapping rings (often spreading)

-        Target-like – concentric circles like a dart-board.
