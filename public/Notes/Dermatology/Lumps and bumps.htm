Features:

-        Rolled pearly edge

-        Telangectasia

-        ± ulceration

 

è Excise!

 

NB. BCCs are malignant – they invade locally!

 

Pyogenic granuloma

 

-        Common benign vascular lesion of the skin and mucosa

-        Red friable nodule often on finger

-        Grows rapidly; should have small pedicle

-        May occur after minor trauma

-        Important differential = nodular melanoma

-        Treatment is curettage and cautery (send to pathology)

 

Do not confuse with…

è Pyodermic gangrenosum

-        Rare serious skin condition involving ulceration; very painful

-        Associated with IBD, RA, Hepatitis, PBC, myeloproliferatyive disorders

-        High dose steroids

 

 

Sebaceous Cyst

 

-        Aka epidermal cyst

-        Painless skin lump often self-resolving

-        Benign proliferation of epidermal cells
