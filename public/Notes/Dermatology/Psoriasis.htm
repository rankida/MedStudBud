Psoriasis

-        Hyperkkeratosis – increased cell turnover/accelerated maturation

-        28 day cycle from basal cell to keratocyte shortened to 4 days

-        T-cell immune reaction

-        Hereditary tendency – 50% +ve FH

-        Multigenic – chromosome 6

 

Clinical features:

 

For exams need to say…

 

-        “salmon pink, silvery scale, well-demarcated plaque”

-        Classically on extensor surfaces

 

-        Nail changes

·        Onycholysis – separation of terminal nail from nail bed

·        Pepper-pot pitting

 

Triggers

 

-        ‘Stress’

-        Lithium, Beta blockers, antimalarials

-        Alcohol

-        Smoking (esp for palmar-planter pustulosis)

-        Rebound after stopping steroids

 

Types of psoriasis

 

·        Chronic Plaque Psoriasis

-        Most common type

 

·        Guttate

-        Occurs weeks after strep throat

-        Most of these patients do not progress onto chronic psoriasis

-        However shows they are genetically sensitive so ↑ risk

 

·        Flexural Psoriasis

-        Not often as scaly

-        May be itchy (DD: eczema)

-        But well demarcated

-        No satellites à not thrush

 

·        Palmar-planter pustulosis

-        Associated with smoking

-        Poor prognosis (even if they stop smoking)

-        Pustiles are sterile

 

·        Erythrodermic

-        >90% of body surface area is red

-        Cx:           

·        ↓barrier function - ↑ infection risk

·        Dehyrdation – need extra 2l/day

·        Hypothermia – need measure core T. fluctuating temp can make it difficult to recognise infection!

·        High output cardiac failure (RHF) – skin is largest organ in the body

·        Hypoalbuminaemia – due to increase skin turnover

 

Management:

 

-        90% improve with sunlight

 

-        Treatment ladder

·        Topical agents

·        Phototherapy

·        2nd lines agents (oral)

·        In-patient treatment – Tar, Dithranol.

·        3rd line agents - Biologicals

 

Topical Agents:

 

-        Calcipitriol (Dovonex)

·        Vit D agonist

·        Revolutionised treatment

·        Max 100mg to avoid systemic absorption à hypercalcaemia

 

Also: tar, dithranol, vit A derivatives, steroids for acute exacerbations.

 

Phototherapy

 

-        Esp used for guttate psoriasis due to large surface area affected

 

-        Narrow band UVB

·        Increasingly used

·        Less carcinogenic and decreased ageing effect

·        Increases risk of BCC x2

 

-        PUVA

·        Used less commonly now

·        Increases risk of SCC x3

·        Can only use if >55, no significant history of previous sun exposure and other treatments have failed

·        Given with oral psoralens

·        Need to protect eyes for 12-24 hours afterwards

 

2nd line – systemic agents

 

Methotrexate

-        Given ONCE A WEEK (with folic acid 3 days later)

-        Cannot drink alcohol while taking

-        Also teratogenic (men and women for 6 months after) – need good contraception

 

Retinoids

-        Eg Acitretin

-        Commonly used in men or working age

-        Teratogenic in women for 2 years – AVOID!

-        Need to monitor LFTs and fasting lipids at first

-        Takes 8 weeks to work

 

Ciclosporin

-        Given in short 8 week courses (due to risk of nephrotoxicity)

-        So effective it is almost cruel!

-        Used for flare-ups, prior to life events, and to try to induce temporary remission in some patients

-        Also used in women with severe disease who are wishing to get pregnant

-        Has largely replaced the need for in patient treatment for many patients

-        Need to monitor U+Es

-        s/e: immunosupression – can ↑ risk of cancer

·        need normal smear prior to starting treatment

-        Also monitor BP for hypertension

 

3rd Line – Biologicals

 

-        Sub-cut anti-TNF therapy

-        V effective but very expensive

-        s/e: injection site reaction, flu like symptoms, anaphylaxis, immunosuppression (military TB). ↑ risk of demyelination. Long term safety still being evaluated.
