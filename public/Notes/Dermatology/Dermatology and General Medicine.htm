Pyodermic gangrenosum

 

-        Rare serious skin condition involving ulceration; very painful

-        Associated with IBD, RA, Hepatitis, PBC, myeloproliferatyive disorders

-        High dose steroids

 

Vasculitides:      

 

RA, SLE, Wegner’s Granulomatosis, Polyarteritis nodosa, churg-strauss syndrome

 

Granulomatous disease:

 

Wegner’s, Sarcoidosis, TB, syphilis.

 

Henoch-Schönlein Purpura

 

-        Occurs post strep URTI in young people

-        IgA auto-immune vasculitis Proteinuria, haematuria

-        Purpura, acute arthritis, abdominal pain, nephritis, orchitis, fever, malaise

-        Rash affects legs and buttocks

-        Usu self-resolving but may respond to steroids

-        Can have serious complications

-        Need to be followed up to ensure not long term nephritis and kidney damage

 

Sarcoidosis

 

-        In dermatology the colour purple =  lymphocytes

·        Think granulomas - sarcoid, wegner’s, TB, syphilis

·        Or lichen planus

 

-        Erythema nodosum

-        Most common presenting complaint in sarcoidosis

-        à CXR everyone with erythema nodosum

·        Bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy?

§  If present, repeat at 1 month - ?interstitial lung disease

§  Rx: Oral steroids

 

Ix: ACE ↑, Ca↑

 

Lupus pernio  

-        Cutaneous manifestation of sarcoid; often presents late in the disease manifestation. Red purple bulbar nose (it looks like rhinophyma of acne rosacea however it is more common in women)

-        Rx: steroids

 

Discoid lupus

 

-        Photosensitive SCARRING rash

-        Usu NOT associated with systemic lupus

-        Rx: high factor sunblock and potent steroids

-        More benign than systemic lupus but can be very disfiguring

 

Vitiligo

 

-        Symmetrical DEpigmentation

·        Note vitiligo is depigmentation NOT hypopigmentation

-        Rx: sunblock and make up

·        Sunblock improves cosmetic appearance and decreases cancer risk

-        In dark skinned people, can use steroids and phototherapy (phototherapy increases their risk of developing skin cancer in their hypopigmented skin, however can be effective treatment). Also topical tacrolimus.

 

If asymmetrical hypopigmentation à LEPROSY!

 

Syphilis

 

-        1º Syphilis – painless ulcer (lasts 2-6 weeks)

·        Often missed by women, homosexual men or if oral lesion

è many people present with 2º syphilis

 

-        2º syphilis

·        Lymphadenopathy

·        Sore throat

·        Heterogenous rash – can look like anything however must consider syphilis in any rash that extends onto the PALMS or SOLEs

·        Fever, lethargy, arthralgia, patchy hair loss

·        ↑↑ ESR

 

If missed, the symptoms spontaneously resolve in 6 weeks.

The bacteria then enters a patent phase and may not re-present for many years

 

-        Tertiary syphilis

·        Depends which organ becomes infected

·        Neurospyhilis – stroke, dementia, blindness, deafness, peripheral neuropathy, paralysis

·        CV – aortitis, AAA, aortic regurgitation, angina

 

Ix: serology for syphilis

 

-        Rx: Penicillin; azithromycin

-        Should be done by GUM team

-        Screen for other STIs

-        Contact tracing

 

Note: ~70% of syphilis is initially misdiagnosed!

 

Lymphoedema

 

-        Decreased lymphatic drainage à accumulation of interstitial fluid with high molecular weight proteins

-        Common complication of surgery (breast cancer axillary clearance) radiotherapy

-        Debilitating progressive condition

-        Initially pitting oedema however inflammatory reaction is triggered à fibrooblasts à brawny non-pitting oedema

-        à ↓immune function à ↑ risk of chronic infection

-        The overlying skin becomes thickened, warty, peau d’orange and can ulcerate.

-        Rx: compression garments, elevate, weight loss, surgery
